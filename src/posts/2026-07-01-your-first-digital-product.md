Most photographers believe they need to invent something before they can sell online. A course, an app, some big idea.

You do not need any of that.

If you have been shooting for a few years, you already built tools for yourself. Presets you refined over hundreds of edits. A culling workflow. A pricing sheet. A client email sequence. You made these things to solve your own problems, and other photographers have the exact same problems.

## The test I use

Before I turn anything into a product, I ask one question: did I build this because I needed it, and did it save me real time or make my work better?

If the answer is yes, someone will pay for it. My Color Grading Manual started as my own editing system. I never planned it as a product. It became one because the problem it solved was not unique to me.

## Start smaller than you think

Your first product should solve one problem. One. A preset pack that fixes skin tones in mixed light. A guide for posing couples who feel awkward. A checklist for shooting a wedding solo.

Small products are easier to make, easier to explain, and easier to sell. You can always build the big thing later, on top of proof that people want what you know.

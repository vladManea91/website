I did not quit weddings because the work was bad. The work was good. The calendar was full.

I quit because of a simple calculation. Every Saturday I sold was a Saturday I could never buy back. My kids were growing and I was editing galleries at midnight for clients I would never see again.

## The trap of being fully booked

Being booked out feels like winning. It is actually a ceiling. Your income has a hard limit: the number of weekends in a year multiplied by your rate. To earn more, you raise prices or work more. Both paths end in the same place.

A digital product has no ceiling. I made my Color Grading Manual once. It has sold to over 1,000 photographers while I was sleeping, driving to Denmark with my family, or shooting only the projects I actually wanted.

## You do not have to quit everything

I still pick up a camera. The difference is that now I choose when. Client work became optional, and optional work is the best kind.

If you are fully booked and still feel stuck, that feeling is information. Listen to it.

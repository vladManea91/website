Artificial intelligence has fundamentally changed how people consume information.

Not long ago, someone would buy a digital product and watch every lesson from beginning to end.

Today, the experience looks very different.

People purchase a course, copy the transcript into ChatGPT or Claude, ask for a summary, extract the most important points, and skip the entire learning process.

That shift is forcing creators to rethink what makes a digital product valuable.

If AI can generate the same information in seconds, what are people actually willing to pay for?

The answer isn't more information.

It's personalization, strategy, and experience.

Information Has Become a Commodity

We're living in a world where AI can answer almost any question.

If you know how to write good prompts, you can generate:

Marketing ideas
Launch strategies
Sales pages
Email sequences
Business plans
Content calendars

Many digital products that sold for $27 or $97 just a few years ago can now be recreated with a single prompt.

That's because information itself is no longer scarce.

The internet is full of it.

AI simply makes it easier to organize and access.

Why Most Digital Products No Longer Feel Valuable

Think about what happens after someone buys a course today.

Instead of watching every lesson, they often:

Download the transcript.
Paste it into ChatGPT or Claude.
Ask for a summary.
Request the key takeaways.
Generate an action plan.

Within minutes, they've extracted everything they believe they need.

Whether that's the best way to learn is another discussion, but it's becoming increasingly common.

This means creators can't rely solely on selling information anymore.

Information alone is becoming replaceable.

The Real Problem Isn't Lack of Information

Ironically, most creators don't struggle because they lack knowledge.

They struggle because they have too much of it.

Someone interested in launching a digital product might purchase:

One course.
Then another.
Then another.

They watch YouTube videos.

They read articles.

They consume newsletters.

Eventually, they end up with dozens of launch strategies.

Instead of feeling confident, they become overwhelmed.

Every creator teaches a slightly different framework.

Every expert recommends a different approach.

The result?

They never launch.

Not because they don't know enough.

Because they no longer know which advice to follow.

AI Can Combine Knowledge—But Not Experience

Artificial intelligence is excellent at organizing information.

For example, you can:

Collect twenty YouTube videos.
Extract every transcript.
Upload them into AI.
Ask for a complete launch strategy.

The result will likely be impressive.

You'll receive a detailed action plan based on hundreds of hours of educational content.

But there's still something missing.

Experience.

AI can summarize what other people teach.

It cannot replace the lessons learned from actually building businesses, launching products, testing offers, and solving real-world problems.

That's where experienced creators still provide enormous value.

The Future Isn't Information—It's Personalized Strategy

As AI continues improving, digital products themselves are changing.

Today, a digital product can take many forms:

PDFs
Video courses
Webinars
AI-generated documents
Interactive workbooks
Claude Artifacts
Custom GPTs
AI assistants

The format matters less than ever.

What matters is whether the product helps someone solve their specific problem.

Generic advice is becoming easier to replace.

Personalized strategy is becoming more valuable.

Why Communities Continue Growing

One of the biggest trends in online education is the rise of communities.

People aren't joining communities simply to access more information.

They're joining because they want:

Feedback.
Accountability.
Support.
Real conversations.
Someone to think through problems with them.

AI can answer questions.

It can't fully replace the experience of working directly with someone who's already solved the problem you're facing.

That's why coaching, communities, and personalized guidance continue becoming more valuable even as AI becomes more powerful.

From Courses to Personalized Game Plans

Instead of creating another generic course, imagine building something different.

Imagine creating a personalized game plan.

Rather than teaching everyone the same framework, the system first learns about the individual.

For example, it asks questions like:

How many hours can you work every week?
How much experience do you have?
What's your niche?
What type of product do you want to launch?
What's your timeline?
What's your current audience size?

Those answers completely change the strategy.

Because no two creators build businesses under the same circumstances.

Every Creator Starts From a Different Place

No two businesses are identical.

One creator might:

Work full time.
Have two children.
Have five hours per week available.

Another might:

Work on their business full time.
Have no children.
Be able to dedicate forty hours every week.

Some creators have years of experience.

Others are complete beginners.

Some have large audiences.

Others are starting with just a few hundred followers.

Even if two people sell in the same niche, they require different strategies.

A one-size-fits-all course simply can't account for all those differences.

Marketing Isn't Universal

Different audiences respond to different marketing.

For example, I've seen photographers behave differently from business creators.

Photography customers often respond well to lower-priced impulse purchases.

Business creators may be much more likely to purchase higher-ticket coaching after entering your ecosystem.

The products are different.

The audiences are different.

The buying behavior is different.

That means the marketing strategy also needs to be different.

Personalization matters.

Research Before You Build

One of the most powerful applications of AI isn't writing content.

It's conducting research.

Instead of guessing what people want, AI can analyze discussions across platforms like:

Reddit
Facebook Groups
Etsy
Forums
Social media comments

The goal isn't to copy existing products.

The goal is understanding:

What people are already buying.
Which products disappointed them.
Why they weren't satisfied.
How much they paid.
What problems remain unsolved.

That research dramatically increases the probability of building a product people actually want.

A Better Way to Launch

Many creators build products backwards.

They come up with an idea first.

Then they create:

Content.
Waitlists.
Email sequences.
Automations.
Landing pages.

Only after weeks—or months—of work do they discover nobody wants the product.

That's incredibly discouraging.

Not because digital products don't work.

But because they built the wrong product.

Validation should happen before creation.

Not after.

What a Personalized Launch Plan Looks Like

Instead of handing every creator the same checklist, a personalized game plan creates a roadmap based on their specific situation.

It can outline:

Week 1

Research the market and validate product demand.

Week 2

Warm up your audience with targeted content.

Week 3

Build your waitlist.

Week 4

Launch your offer.

Each stage is adapted to the creator's experience, available time, audience size, and niche.

Instead of guessing what to do next, they simply execute the next step.

You Don't Need a Massive Audience

One of the biggest myths in the creator economy is that you need hundreds of thousands of followers before launching a product.

That's simply not true.

Smaller creators are successfully launching profitable offers every day.

For example:

A creator with around 2,000 followers can begin building a waitlist for a niche coaching program.

Another creator teaching an incredibly specific skill—such as helping Ukrainians learn Slovak—can successfully build a waitlist, launch a community, and generate recurring monthly revenue.

These businesses don't succeed because of audience size alone.

They succeed because the offer matches the audience's needs.

The Next Generation of Digital Products

The future of digital products isn't endless libraries of videos.

It's intelligent systems that combine:

Research.
Personalization.
Experience.
Strategy.
Accountability.

Instead of selling more information, creators will increasingly sell clarity.

Instead of giving everyone the same roadmap, they'll help each person build the roadmap that's right for them.

AI has made information almost unlimited.

That means the real competitive advantage is no longer knowing more than everyone else.

It's helping people apply the right information to their unique situation.

The creators who embrace that shift won't compete with AI.

They'll use AI to deliver something far more valuable: personalized strategies that help people take action, launch faster, and build profitable businesses with confidence.
